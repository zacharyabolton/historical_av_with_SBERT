Rev E P Smith

Jan 18 1868

Dear Sir

Miss Burke came Saturday night went into Sab Schl yesterday and will take charge of her department in the day school to day. She is truly welcome

We have now 150 day scholars--and still they come--as ever. The beding has not arrived but as Miss Burke has some already with her I doubt whether we shall need it Shall I sell it to some of these Colored people now while it is winter and they need it--if I can get nearly cost.

We might make permanent arrangement for a Mission House I presume but as the colored people are so crotchety here there is no telling how long they will permit Northern Societies to labor for them.

Mrs Davison is doing first rate and we could not now part with her.

The Ladies would live very much the "New York Independent" and we should be glad of the "American Missionary"--no numbers of which have yet been received by us.

Very Respectfully

John Scott
