DE OLE OX KYART
de kingdomes', soshubles', roomies'
chariotable ebber rid in, en he nebber
wuz ez happy ez when he wuz a gee-
hawin' a load er de nayberhood gals
ter a ball er ter a camp-meetin', en
hit mos' bruck his heart ter come
down ter one steer en a single steer
kyart; but dat's freedom. We won'
nebber see de ole times no mo'. My
ole man done dribe his las' yoke er
oxens-done rid in his las' ox-kyart,
fer dey done en eben got a new hearse
up at de village, en dey don' put de
curtains on de ox-kyarts no mo' now,
ner hitch de brack hosses ter hit lak
dey use' ter fer ter dribe de corpses, kaze
de hearse is meked now fer de puppose
er ridin' all de daid."
Aunt Nettie, or Mammy Borry, for
we always called her by her child-
