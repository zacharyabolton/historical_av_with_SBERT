ness. 'Fo' our li'l' Horner come inter
dis worl' er restress en lef' fer de lan'
er promise yo' Ung' Charles wuz a
moughty diffunt man. 'Fo' dat he
wuz a gre't hunter. He had mo' dan
a hund'ud traps whar he use' ter set
uver winter nights, en he had sich
luck wid coons en weasels en ole hyars
en 'possums en all kin's er varments
dat de outsides er we-all's li'l' cabin
wuz kibbered wid skins nailt up ter dry
all de time; but w'en li'l' Horner die
yo' Ung' Charles gin his coon dorgs en
all his traps away. 'Pear lak anyt'ing
dat wuz young en he'pless sot 'im in
min' er li'l' Horner-boy.
